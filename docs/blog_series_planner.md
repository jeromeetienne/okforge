# Design a Blog Series for This Project

I want to create a series of blog posts about this project.

Do **not** write any articles yet. Your goal is to design the strongest possible series first.

This prompt is about **content strategy and structure only**. The writing style, tone, and wording will be handled by another prompt.

## Objectives

Optimize the series to:

* Clearly explain the project.
* Showcase the engineering and design decisions behind it.
* Demonstrate my expertise in AI and software engineering.
* Leave readers with a memorable understanding of both the project and how I think.
* Support my goal of attracting AI consulting work, freelance contracts, and engineering opportunities.

The series should be reusable for **any** project, regardless of its size or technical domain.

---

## Phase 1 — Interview

Before proposing anything, interview me.

Ask whatever questions you need to fully understand:

* the project,
* why it exists,
* the problem it solves,
* who it is for,
* the interesting engineering decisions,
* the trade-offs,
* what makes it different,
* what surprised me while building it,
* the intended readers,
* my goals for publishing,
* the level of technical depth,
* and anything else that would help design an outstanding series.

Keep asking questions until you have enough information. Do **not** propose an outline until then.

---

## Phase 2 — Identify the Story

Once you have enough information, first identify:

* the central story this project tells,
* what makes it interesting,
* what readers are most likely to remember,
* what aspects are worth highlighting,
* and which topics are *not* worth spending articles on.

If there are multiple possible narratives, compare them and recommend the strongest one.

---

## Phase 3 — Design the Series

Design the complete series.

Do **not** assume a specific number of posts.

Instead, recommend the structure that best fits the project, whether that is 2, 5, 10, or more posts.

For each proposed post, provide:

* a working title,
* a short synopsis (2–5 sentences),
* the primary audience,
* what prior knowledge is assumed,
* the key takeaway,
* what this article demonstrates about my expertise,
* why it belongs in the series,
* and how it naturally leads into the next article.

The series should have a clear progression rather than feeling like unrelated articles.

---

## Phase 4 — Review

Critique your own proposal.

Identify:

* weak articles,
* overlapping topics,
* missing topics,
* opportunities for stronger storytelling,
* ways to better demonstrate technical expertise,
* and ways to make the series more valuable to engineers, founders, and hiring managers.

Then produce your final recommended outline.

Throughout this process, challenge my assumptions when appropriate. If you believe there is a stronger structure than the one I seem to expect, explain why and recommend it instead.
